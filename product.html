DACS — Risk Assessment Model

Twelve pillars of crypto risk assessment, backed by 8 patent-pending innovations — quantifying borrower position beyond traditional credit metrics in language the member can actually understand.

1

Wallet Age

Longevity of on-chain presence signals commitment and experience.

2

Diversification

Portfolio spread across assets reduces single-point-of-failure risk.

3

On-Chain Activity

Transaction frequency and patterns demonstrate active asset management.

4

Volatility

Historical price stability of held assets informs collateral resilience.

5

Tax-Position Score (TPSOE)

Patent-pending pillar. Scores the borrower's embedded tax liability and collateral tax efficiency — ranking pledge assets to minimize tax cost at liquidation.

How It Works

  1. Member deposits crypto collateral into a BitGo institutional custody wallet — held directly by the credit union, never by Aetherum.
  2. DACS engine scores the borrower and sets LTV, rate, and margin thresholds.
  3. Credit union issues the loan using existing infrastructure — Aetherum handles the rest.
  4. Automated monitoring manages margin calls, top-ups, and liquidation if needed.

GENIUS Act Ready

The NCUA's first GENIUS Act proposed rule closed its comment period on April 13, 2026. The final implementing regulations are due by July 18, 2026 — establishing the compliance framework for how credit unions participate in the digital asset economy.

Aetherum was built inside this regulatory perimeter. Our ERC-3643 compliance modules, on-chain KYC/AML attestation, DACS risk assessment model, and BitGo institutional custody architecture are designed to operate within NCUA supervision — not around it.

Three things the GENIUS Act framework confirms about how Aetherum is built:

  • On-chain identity: Every borrower receives an ERC-3643 compliant ONCHAINID attestation — KYC verified, jurisdiction gated, and NCUA-audit ready.
  • Custody architecture: Each credit union holds its own institutional custody account. Aetherum is software layer only — no commingled funds, no custodial risk on the platform.
  • Compliance-first infrastructure: DACS scoring, LTV enforcement, and collateral management logic are built to produce the audit trails NCUA examiners will expect.
