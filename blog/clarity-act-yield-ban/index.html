Three Ways Out. Two Are Now Closed.

When a crypto holder needs USD liquidity, they've historically had three options:

Option 1: Sell. Convert BTC or ETH to dollars. Simple—but it triggers a taxable event, surrenders future upside, and is permanent. In a bull market, selling feels like punishing yourself for being right.

Option 2: Earn yield on stablecoins. Park holdings in USDC and let a platform pay you 3–5% to sit still. This was the workaround that let holders stay liquid without selling. The CLARITY Act is closing this door. Passive yield on stablecoins is functionally banned for U.S.-regulated platforms. The deal is done in principle; the markup is April.

Option 3: Borrow against your crypto. Use your BTC, ETH, or other digital assets as collateral, take out a USD loan, keep your position intact, and repay when it suits you. No taxable event. No lost upside. Full liquidity.

Option 3 is the only door that remains open—and regulators didn't just leave it open, they built it into the architecture of the CLARITY Act itself.

From the CLARITY Act

The bill would ensure stablecoins cannot be treated as a credit union or bank deposit, and would clarify that credit unions offering digital asset services are not required to hold assets held in custody as liabilities on their balance sheet—removing one of the last structural barriers to CU-based crypto lending.
